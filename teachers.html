Teach Child Rights in Your Classroom

Free lesson plans, activities and digital tools to help you bring Sri Lankan child rights education into your school — in Sinhala, Tamil and English.

Why Teach Child Rights?

Sri Lanka has ratified the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child. Teaching children about their rights builds confidence, reduces vulnerability to abuse, and creates a generation that understands and respects the law. Justice Kids makes this easy — the platform does the explaining, you guide the discussion.

No preparation needed. Open Justice Kids on a classroom screen or tablet and work through the rights cards together. The quiz handles assessment automatically. Everything is in Sinhala, Tamil and English.

Suggested Lesson Plans

Ages 10–12

🏫 Lesson 1: My Rights at School

Use the Education and Safety rights cards. Discuss caning, bullying and the Zonal Director complaint process. Take the quiz as a class.

⏱ 45 minutes · No materials needed
Ages 12–15

📱 Lesson 2: Online Safety & the Law

Use the Online Safety rights card. Discuss cyberbullying and the Computer Crimes Act. Ask students to use the AI Q&A to ask their own questions.

⏱ 45 minutes · Devices helpful
Ages 13–17

⚖ Lesson 3: What is the Law?

Work through the glossary together. Discuss what a Grama Niladhari does, what the Legal Aid Commission is and when a child goes to Juvenile Court.

⏱ 60 minutes · Discussion based
All ages

🧠 Lesson 4: Rights Quiz Challenge

Split the class into teams. Each team uses one device to take the quiz. First team to a perfect score wins. Download the certificate for each winner.

⏱ 30 minutes · Competitive & fun
Ages 14–17

🗣 Lesson 5: Real Stories, Real Rights

Read the three case stories (Ayesha, Sahan, Nimasha). Discuss: What right was violated? Who helped? What would you do? Role play the scenario.

⏱ 45 minutes · Discussion based
Ages 10–17

👪 Lesson 6: If Something Happens to Me

Discuss safe adults, the NCPA helpline (1929), and the Safe Help Portal. Make sure every child knows the number 1929 before leaving class.

⏱ 30 minutes · Important for all ages

How to Use Justice Kids in Class

  1. Open on a screen — Project acedynamicstrading.github.io/justicekids on your classroom projector or smart board. No login, no app needed.
  2. Select a language — Switch between English, Sinhala and Tamil at the top. You can switch mid-lesson.
  3. Work through the rights cards — Click each card to expand. Read it aloud, pause and discuss. Ask: Has anyone seen this happen? What would you do?
  4. Run the quiz — Take it as a class vote, or let students use devices individually. Show the score breakdown at the end to review wrong answers.
  5. Celebrate perfect scores — Any student who scores 6/6 can download a personalised certificate with their name on it.
  6. End with the helplines — Always close by pointing out the emergency numbers (1929, 119) at the bottom of the page.

Reporting a Concern

As a teacher, if a child discloses abuse or you suspect a child is in danger, you are a mandatory reporter under Sri Lankan law. Please contact:

  • NCPA Hotline: 1929 — 24 hours, free, confidential
  • Police: 119 — immediate danger
  • Women & Children Ministry: 1938
  • Department of Probation & Child Care Services — your local office

If a child in your school needs free legal help for their family, direct them to LexLK — free legal consultations for all Sri Lankans.
