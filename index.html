🦁 Meet Leo

Leo's Story

Leo is a lion cub just like you. This is his story — and it could be yours too. Are you ready to be a hero?

1
Chapter One · The Problem

Leo walked sadly alone...

Every morning, Leo walked sadly to school. His best friend was in trouble — something unfair had happened. Leo wanted to help but didn't know how.

He didn't know that the law could protect them both.

"My friend needed help. I wanted to help. But I didn't know where to start."
2
Chapter Two · The Discovery

Leo finds children's rights and the SOS button!

One day Leo saw the JusticeKids board at his school gate. He discovered something incredible — he had rights. Real rights, protected by Sri Lankan law.

And he discovered the SOS button — a way to call for help instantly. Leo had found the tools that could save his friend.

"I have rights! And there's a button that can bring help. I'm going to use it!"
3
Chapter Three · The Action

Leo uses the SOS and gets help! 🆘🚀

When his friend needed help most, Leo pressed the SOS button. His location was sent to a trained field officer. Help launched like a rocket — it was on the way!

Leo learned that one button and the knowledge of your rights can change everything.

"I pressed it. Someone came. My friend wasn't alone anymore."
4
Chapter Four · The Power

Leo saves his friend — knowledge is power! 💪

Leo saved his friend — not with fists, but with knowledge. He knew his rights. He knew who to call. He knew the law was on their side.

The more Leo had learned, the more powerful he became. Knowledge of rights and laws is the greatest superpower a child can have.

"I didn't need to be big or strong. I just needed to know my rights."
5
Chapter Five · The Help

Trusted adults were there for them 💙

The field officer helped Leo get legal support for his friend. Doctors, teachers and paramedics were all there. The NCPA helpline 1929 was free, 24 hours a day.

Leo realised that trusted adults are not scary — they are the ones who protect you when you reach out.

"There are people who care about my friend too. We just needed to find them."
6
Chapter Six · The Hero

Leo runs home — a hero! 🏆

Leo ran home with joy in his heart. He found his parents and told them everything. "Mum! Dad! I used my rights and I saved my friend! The law was on our side!"

His parents hugged him tight. Leo had become a JusticeKids Rights Champion.

"You can be a hero too — just like Leo. Are you ready? Your rights are waiting for you!"

📚 Word Glossary

What Do These Words Mean?

Legal words can sound scary. Here they are explained simply.

NCPA
National Child Protection Authority — a government body that protects children from abuse. Free helpline: 1929 (24 hours).
UNCRC
United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child — an international agreement signed by Sri Lanka giving all children specific rights.
Penal Code
Sri Lanka's main book of criminal laws. Hurting a child is a crime under the Penal Code.
Grama Niladhari
A government officer responsible for a village. They can help families in need and connect people to social services.
Legal Aid Commission
Provides free lawyers to people who cannot afford one — including children. Phone: 011 243 3618.
Juvenile Court
A special court for children under 16. Focuses on the child's wellbeing, not just punishment.
Medico-Legal Certificate
A document written by a doctor that records injuries. Can be used as evidence in court.
Zonal Director of Education
A government officer responsible for schools in your area. They can investigate complaints about schools.
