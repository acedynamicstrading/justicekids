What You Need to Know About Justice Kids

A safe, educational platform that teaches Sri Lankan children about their legal rights — and provides emergency help when they need it most.

What is Justice Kids?

Justice Kids is a free educational platform developed by Ace Dynamics Trading under the Justice LK National Digital Justice Access Programme. It is designed to teach children aged 10–17 about their legal rights under Sri Lankan law in simple, friendly language — in Sinhala, Tamil and English.

Safe Help Portal

A private, guided system for children facing abuse, bullying or other serious situations.

Is It Safe for My Child?

No personal information is collected from children. Justice Kids does not ask for names, school names, addresses, NIC numbers or any identifying information. All AI interactions are completely anonymous.

  • Children can use the platform without creating an account
  • Quiz results are logged anonymously for statistical purposes only
  • The AI Q&A system answers questions without storing the child's identity
  • No advertising, no tracking, no profiling of children
  • The platform is ad-free — no third parties can advertise to your child

The Emergency SOS Feature

The Safe Help Portal includes an SOS emergency button. We want to be fully transparent with parents about how this works:

When a child presses the SOS button: Their GPS location is captured and sent to the Justice LK safety team. A phone number field is shown — this is optional and only used to call the child back. Location updates are sent every 30 seconds until the child presses "I am safe now."

  • The SOS feature is designed for genuine emergencies only
  • Location data is used solely to respond to the emergency — it is not stored permanently
  • The safety team will attempt to contact the child and connect them with appropriate authorities
  • Emergency hotlines (NCPA 1929, Police 119) are always shown alongside the SOS button

If your child has accidentally pressed SOS, they can press "I am safe now" to stop tracking and notify the team.

How You Can Help

  • Explore together — go through the rights cards with your child so you can discuss them
  • Take the quiz together — it is a great conversation starter about the law
  • Talk about the emergency features — make sure your child knows the SOS button is for real emergencies
  • Know the helplines — NCPA 1929, Police 119, Women & Children 1938
